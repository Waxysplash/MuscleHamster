# Muscle Hamster — Character Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** Feb 7, 2026

> This guide defines the visual style, personality, and asset requirements for the Muscle Hamster character. All artists and designers should follow these specifications to ensure consistency across the app.

---

## 1. Reference & Inspiration

**Primary Reference:** Cute cartoon hamster with fitness theme
- Style: Rounded, friendly cartoon illustration
- Body: Plump, orange/golden hamster with soft curves
- Accessories: Fitness gear (headband, wristbands)
- Mood: Energetic but approachable, never intimidating

**Inspirational Keywords:**
- Kawaii fitness
- Friendly gym buddy
- Cozy + active
- Chibi proportions (large head, small body)

---

## 2. Core Design Principles

### 2.1 Personality Through Art

The hamster must embody the app's core values:

| Principle | Visual Expression |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Warmth over pressure** | Soft edges, rounded shapes, welcoming expressions |
| **Gentle accountability** | Hungry = sad eyes, NOT angry face |
| **Playful energy** | Bouncy poses, sparkle effects, cute accessories |
| **Approachable fitness** | Light exercise gear, not hardcore bodybuilder |

### 2.2 What to Avoid

- Aggressive or intimidating expressions
- Overly muscular/bodybuilder proportions
- Angry, disappointed, or judgmental faces
- Sharp edges or harsh lines
- Realistic hamster anatomy (keep it stylized)

---

## 3. Character Specifications

### 3.1 Base Design

```
HEAD-TO-BODY RATIO: 1:1.2 (slightly larger head)
OVERALL SHAPE: Soft oval/rounded rectangle
EYE STYLE: Large, expressive, with highlights
EARS: Small, rounded, perky
CHEEKS: Prominent hamster pouches (signature feature)
PAWS: Stubby, cute "mitten" style
TAIL: Tiny, barely visible
```

### 3.2 Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |

### 3.3 Line Work

- **Outline weight:** Medium (not too thin, not too thick)
- **Outline color:** Slightly darker than fill color (NOT black)
- **Style:** Soft, slightly tapered lines
- **Corners:** Always rounded, never sharp

## 4. Emotional States

The hamster has four primary states tied to user activity. Each state needs distinct visual treatment.

### 4.1 Happy/Fed (Workout Completed)

**Trigger:** User completed today's workout

**Visual Elements:**
- Big smile, eyes closed or sparkling
- Cheeks slightly puffed (just ate!)
- Energetic pose (jumping, flexing, celebrating)
- Optional: Sparkle/star effects around character
- Optional: Sweat drop (cute, post-workout glow)

**Expression Keywords:** Joyful, satisfied, proud, energetic

**Poses to Create:**
- Jumping with arms up
- Flexing tiny arm muscles (cute, not serious)
- Giving thumbs up
- Happy dance

---

### 4.2 Chillin' (Rest Day)

**Trigger:** Rest day, user completed check-in

**Visual Elements:**
- Relaxed, content expression
- Half-lidded eyes (cozy, not tired)
- Lounging or seated pose
- Optional: Cup of tea, blanket, or cozy props
- Soft, muted lighting feel

**Expression Keywords:** Relaxed, content, cozy, peaceful

**Poses to Create:**
- Sitting with legs out, leaning back
- Lying on belly, chin on paws
- Stretching lazily
- Wrapped in blanket

---

### 4.3 Hungry (Scheduled Workout Not Done Yet)

**Trigger:** It's a workout day and user hasn't worked out yet

**Visual Elements:**
- Slightly sad/hopeful eyes (NOT angry)
- Paws near mouth or belly
- Smaller body posture (waiting, not demanding)
- Optional: Small rumbling stomach lines
- Optional: Empty food bowl nearby

**Expression Keywords:** Hopeful, waiting, gentle longing, patient

**CRITICAL:** This state must feel like an invitation, NOT guilt. The hamster is hungry and hopeful, looking forward to being fed—never accusatory or disappointed.

**Poses to Create:**
- Sitting, looking up hopefully
- Paws on empty bowl
- Gentle "waiting" pose

---

### 4.4 Sad/Neglected (Streak Broken)

**Trigger:** User missed a check-in day (streak reset)

**Visual Elements:**
- Droopy ears
- Downturned mouth (subtle, not dramatic)
- Watery eyes (NOT crying dramatically)
- Slightly slumped posture
- Muted colors (optional: slight desaturation)

**Expression Keywords:** Lonely, missing you, hopeful for return

**CRITICAL:** Even in the saddest state, the hamster should feel like it misses the user—NOT that it's punishing them. The goal is "I missed you!" not "You failed me!"

**Poses to Create:**
- Sitting alone, looking at door/screen
- Slight slump, paws together
- Looking at old workout gear hopefully

## 5. Growth Stages

The hamster grows through four life stages based on cumulative workout completions and streak milestones.

### 5.1 Baby (Starting Stage)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Extra large head-to-body ratio (1:0.8)
- Bigger eyes relative to face
- Rounder, softer features
- Smaller size overall (60% of adult)
- Wobbly, inexperienced poses
- Simple/no accessories

**Personality:** Curious, clumsy, adorable, learning

---

### 5.2 Juvenile (Early Progress)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Slightly more balanced proportions (1:1)
- Still cute but more confident
- Size: 75% of adult
- Can wear basic accessories
- More coordinated poses

**Personality:** Eager, growing confidence, enthusiastic

---

### 5.3 Adult (Established User)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Standard proportions (1:1.2)
- Full size (100%)
- All accessories available
- Confident, varied poses
- Clear personality expression

**Personality:** Confident, supportive, encouraging

---

### 5.4 Mature (Veteran User)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Same proportions as adult
- Subtle "wisdom" indicators (optional):
  - Slightly fluffier fur
  - Confident stance
  - Premium/special default accessories
- Optional: Subtle glow or aura effect

**Personality:** Wise, proud, accomplished, nurturing

## 6. Customization Assets

### 6.1 Outfits

All outfits must work across all growth stages (scaled appropriately).

**Categories:**

| Category | Examples | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Fitness Wear | Headbands, wristbands, sweatbands, tank tops | Default theme |
| Casual | T-shirts, hoodies, caps | Everyday looks |
| Sporty | Jerseys, athletic shorts, sneakers | Sport-specific |
| Seasonal | Holiday hats, scarves, sunglasses | Time-limited (future) |
| Premium/Rare | Superhero capes, costumes, sparkle effects | High point cost |

**Technical Requirements:**
- Each outfit piece on separate layer
- Consistent anchor points for all growth stages
- Color variants where applicable
- Preview thumbnail for shop

---

### 6.2 Poses

**Base Poses Required (All States):**
- Standing neutral
- Sitting neutral
- Action pose (per state)

**Purchasable Poses:**

| Pose Type | Examples |
|-----------|----------|

| Victory | Medal pose, trophy hold, fist pump |
| Cute | Waving, blowing kiss, peace sign, sleeping |
| Silly | Upside down, tongue out, spinning |

---

### 6.3 Enclosure Items

Items for decorating the hamster's living space.

**Categories:**

| Category | Examples | Sizes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Fitness Equipment | Tiny dumbbells, yoga mat, exercise ball | S, M |
| Furniture | Bean bag, hamster wheel, bed, couch | M, L |
| Decorations | Plants, posters, trophies, medals | S |
| Background Elements | Gym wall, park backdrop, bedroom | Full BG |

**Placement Rules:**
- Items on grid system for easy placement
- Z-order layers (background, mid, foreground)
- Hamster always visible and prominent

## 7. Animation Guidelines (Future)

For animated states (if/when implemented):

### 7.1 Idle Animations

- **Happy:** Gentle bounce, occasional arm wave
- **Chillin':** Slow breathing, occasional yawn
- **Hungry:** Belly rub, hopeful look around
- **Sad:** Slow ear droop, occasional sigh

### 7.2 Transition Animations

- Workout complete → Happy celebration (3-5 sec)
- Rest day check-in → Cozy settle (2-3 sec)
- State change → Smooth morph (1 sec)

### 7.3 Frame Rates

- Idle loops: 12 fps (smooth but efficient)
- Celebrations: 24 fps (high energy)
- Transitions: 24 fps

## 8. Asset Delivery Specifications

### 8.1 File Formats

| Asset Type | Format | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Static images | PNG | Transparent background |
| Animations | Lottie JSON or sprite sheets | TBD based on implementation |
| Source files | Figma / Adobe Illustrator | Layered, organized |

### 8.2 Resolution

- Design at **3x** resolution for Retina displays
- Export at 1x, 2x, 3x for iOS asset catalogs
- Hamster on home screen: ~300x300pt (900x900px at 3x)

### 8.3 Naming Convention

```
hamster_[stage]_[state]_[pose]_[variant].png

Examples:
hamster_adult_happy_jumping_default.png
hamster_baby_hungry_sitting_default.png
hamster_juvenile_chillin_lounging_default.png
```

## 10. Tone Reminder

Every visual decision should pass this test:

> "Does this make the user feel warmly invited to move their body, or does it make them feel judged for not doing so?"

The hamster is a **supportive friend**, not a **disappointed parent**.

When in doubt, choose:
- Softer over harsher
- Hopeful over accusatory
- Inviting over demanding
- Cute over intense
